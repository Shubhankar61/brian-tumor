Brain Tumor


a brain tumor is an abnormal mass or growth of cells in the brain. These cells multiply uncontrollably, forming a lump. Brain tumors can be either benign (non-cancerous) or malignant (cancerous). Benign Tumor: Usually slow-growing. Often confined to a specific area. Generally considered less harmful and may not spread to other parts of the body. Malignant Tumor: Tends to grow faster. Can invade surrounding tissues. Has the potential to spread to other parts of the brain or the body.


Symptoms


Common symptoms of brain tumors may include headaches, nausea, changes in vision, seizures, and difficulty with coordination or balance. However, the symptoms can vary based on the location and size of the tumor. Diagnosis involves imaging tests like MRI or CT scans, and treatment options may include surgery, radiation therapy, or chemotherapy, depending on the type and severity of the tumor.


Project Benfit

This project holds significant benefits for the field of healthcare by leveraging advanced technology to enhance early diagnosis and intervention. By utilizing artificial intelligence and deep learning models to analyze medical imaging data, the project aims to provide a rapid and accurate assessment of brain tumors. Early detection of brain tumors is crucial for timely medical intervention, leading to improved patient outcomes, increased treatment success rates, and potentially reducing the overall healthcare burden associated with advanced-stage cases. This project has the potential to contribute to more efficient and cost-effective healthcare practices, ultimately enhancing the quality of life for individuals affected by brain tumors.
